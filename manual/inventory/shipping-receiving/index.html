Shipping & receiving

Move goods in and out with shipments and receipts.

This page is about the two moments when physical goods actually cross your dock: stuff coming in from a supplier (receiving), and finished goods going out to a customer (shipping). In Carbon these aren't loose warehouse chores — each one points back to a document you already created. A receipt settles a purchase order line; a shipment settles a sales order line and feeds the invoice. Do them right and your on-hand counts, your costs, and your customer's bill all stay in sync on their own.

Where this fits. Receiving is the back half of purchasing — you raised a PO, now the goods arrive. Shipping is the back half of selling — you made the parts on a job, now they leave. Both flows read and write the stock you keep in your locations and bins.

Two flows, one idea

A receipt and a shipment are mirror images of each other. One adds to inventory, the other subtracts from it. Both work the same way: you start from a source document, confirm the real-world quantities, optionally record which physical material moved (its lot or batch), and then post — the moment Carbon commits the change to your books.

Loading diagram...

We'll use Acme's hero order to walk both ends. The A36 steel for the Counterweight Hooks arrives on PO000057 from Ryerson — that's receiving. The 500 finished hooks leave for Radiant Aerospace against SO000069 — that's shipping.

"Post" is the verb that matters. Up until you post, a receipt or shipment is a draft you can still edit or delete — nothing has changed in inventory. Posting is the irreversible commit: stock levels move, costs land, and downstream documents update. Get the numbers right before you post.

Flow 1 — Receiving against a purchase order

When a truck backs up to the dock with material you ordered, you create a receipt against the matching purchase order. Carbon already knows what you expected — the part, the quantity, the price — so receiving is mostly confirming reality and noting any differences.

For the hero thread, Acme ordered 54,001 LB of A36 Steel Plate from Ryerson on PO000057. Here's the open PO line waiting to be received.

Open the PO and start a receipt

Go to Purchasing → Purchase Orders, open PO000057, and choose Receive (or start from Inventory → Receipts → New and pick the PO). Carbon copies the open lines onto a fresh receipt so you don't re-type anything. The receipt starts as a Draft — safe to edit.

A purchase order has to be issued to the supplier before you can receive against it. PO000057 is currently Needs Approval — in a real flow it would be approved and sent first. You can't receive goods against a PO that's still a draft.

Confirm the quantities that actually arrived

The receipt pre-fills each line with the quantity still outstanding. Edit the received quantity to match what the packing slip and your scale say. Three common cases:

  • Exact — the truck brought all 54,001 LB. Leave the line as-is.
  • Short — only part of the order came. Receive what arrived; the PO line stays open for the rest (a partial receipt), and you can receive the balance on a later delivery.
  • Over — more came than you ordered. Carbon flags the overage so you can accept it or send it back, per your purchasing rules.

Record the lot or batch for tracked items

A36 Steel Plate is batch-tracked (requiresBatchTracking: true). That means Carbon won't just add "20,000 lb of steel" to a faceless pile — it wants to know which heat of steel this is, so that months later you can answer "exactly which material went into Radiant's hooks?" Type the supplier's heat/lot number into the batch field on the line.

Serial vs batch. Carbon's itemTrackingType decides how granular this gets. Inventory items (like the finished hook) just track a count. Batch items track by lot — one number for the whole delivery. Serial items track every single unit individually. You only see a batch or serial field when the item asks for one, so for most parts this step simply doesn't appear. More in Locations & stock.

Tell Carbon where it goes

Each received line needs a home: a location and a shelf (bin). Acme's steel lives at the Headquarters location in bin R02-B01-01 — its default — so the receipt suggests that automatically. Change it if you're stocking it somewhere else.

Post the receipt

When the numbers and batch are right, choose Post. At that instant:

  • On-hand stock of A36 Steel Plate goes up by the received quantity in bin R02-B01-01.
  • The PO line's quantityReceived updates, moving the PO toward Received / To Invoice.
  • Carbon posts the value to your Inventory account and to GR/IR Clearing (the holding account between goods-received and invoice-received) — see Accounting overview.

The material is now real stock the job can pull from. The Counterweight Hook job needs 40 LB per piece × 500 = 20,000 LB, and it now has steel to consume.

The same dock handles services and outside work. If a PO line is for an Outside Operation (you sent parts out for, say, anodizing), receiving it back is the same motion — it tells Carbon the subcontracted step is done and the parts are home.

Flow 2 — Shipping against a sales order

Once the parts are made and inspected, you send them. A shipment records what physically left the building, against the sales order (and the job behind it). The hero job J000097 finished all 500 Counterweight Hooks and received them into inventory (quantityReceivedToInventory: 500); now those 500 ship to Radiant Aerospace on SO000069.

Start a shipment from the order

Open SO000069 under Sales → Sales Orders and choose Ship (or go to Inventory → Shipments → New and select the order). Carbon creates a shipment and brings the order lines across. As with receipts, it begins as a Draft you can adjust before posting.

Pick the finished goods

Picking is choosing which stock fills the shipment. For the hooks, Carbon pulls from the job's finished-goods bin (the job stored its output in storage unit 8-1-H). Confirm the quantity to ship on each line — here, all 500 EA.

If a part is batch- or serial-tracked, you pick the specific lots/serials here, and Carbon records them on the shipment. That closes the traceability loop: you'll be able to trace this shipment back to the exact heat of A36 steel you received in Flow 1.

You can only ship what you have. Carbon won't let you ship more than is on hand in the picking location. If the job isn't finished or the finished goods haven't been received into inventory yet, the quantity you can pick is capped. Short shipments are fine — the SO line stays open for the balance.

Choose a shipping method

A shipping method is the carrier and service the goods leave on — it prints on paperwork and tells everyone how the parts are travelling. Acme has ten methods configured, from carrier overnight to a customer picking up themselves. Pick the one that matches the order.

A pallet of 500 steel hooks is heavy freight, not a parcel — so LTL (Less Than Truckload) or FTL (Full Truckload) is the right call here, not UPS Ground. The method you choose is also where a shipping cost rides along: SO000069 carries $250 of freight, which shows up on the invoice but not in the goods subtotal.

Add tracking details (optional)

If the carrier gives you a tracking number, add it to the shipment along with package counts and weights. It's optional, but it travels with the record so anyone looking at the order later can answer "where is it?" without leaving Carbon.

Post the shipment

Choose Post to commit. At that instant a cascade fires:

  • The 500 hooks leave inventory from the picking bin.
  • The SO line's quantitySent updates to 500 of 500, and because the whole line shipped it's flagged sentComplete — the order moves toward Completed.
  • The job's quantityShipped updates to 500.
  • A shipment record is created (sh_7injMjdu1xyZqTVFFrjauv) — the link the invoice reads from next.

What posting the shipment sets in motion

The whole point of tying shipments to the sales order is what happens after you post. You recorded one fact — "500 hooks left the dock" — and Carbon updates everything that depends on it.

Because the shipment is posted, Acme can now raise the sales invoice. Carbon builds AR000025 straight from what shipped — 500 EA at $73.14, plus the $250 freight — so the bill matches the truck, with no re-keying. The invoice references this exact shipment (shipmentId: sh_7injMjdu1xyZqTVFFrjauv), and once Radiant pays it lands as Paid. That is the receiving-to-shipping arc closing into cash.

One number, entered once. You never told Carbon "the order is complete" or "bill the customer." You said "500 hooks shipped," and the sales order line, the job, the shipment record, and the invoice all updated themselves. That's the system-of-record payoff.
