Carbon in 15 minutes

Follow one real order from quote to cash — the whole Carbon flow, end to end, using a real Acme part.

The fastest way to understand Carbon is to watch one order move through it. This page follows a single real part — a Counterweight Hook that Acme makes for Radiant Aerospace — from the first price request all the way to a paid invoice. You don't need to do anything here; just read along and you'll have the whole map. Every later section zooms in on one of these stops.

Loading diagram...

Meet the part. The Counterweight Hook (part number CWH-A36-15) is a heavy steel hook flame-cut from 1-inch A36 steel plate. Radiant Aerospace orders them 500 at a time. Carbon calls anything you make or buy an item — this one is a Part that Acme makes (rather than buys).

Step 1 — A customer asks for a price (the Quote)

It starts when Radiant Aerospace asks "what would 500 of these cost?" In Carbon you answer with a quote: you add a line for the part, the quantity, and a price. Carbon adds up the cost of materials and shop time behind the scenes so you can price the work with confidence.

When the customer says yes, you convert the quote into a sales order with one click — no re-typing. Carbon keeps the quote and the order linked, so you can always trace a job back to the price you promised.

Quotes are also where Carbon's configurators shine — for made-to-order parts the customer's choices (size, material, finish) can drive the price automatically. More in Quoting & estimating.

Step 2 — You win the work (the Sales Order)

The sales order is the customer's commitment: who's buying, what, how many, when it's due, and at what price. It's the anchor everything else hangs off — the job that makes the part, the shipment that sends it, and the invoice that bills for it all point back here.

Notice the fulfillment method on the line: Make to Order. That single setting tells Carbon this part isn't sitting on a shelf — it has to be made — so the next step happens almost automatically.

Step 3 — Carbon knows how it's made (the Method)

Every part you make has a method: a recipe with two halves.

  • The bill of materials (BOM) — what it's made of.
  • The routing — the steps to make it, and where each step happens.

For the Counterweight Hook, the recipe is short: take a slab of 1-inch A36 steel plate, then run it through three shop operations.

Each hook needs 40 lb of steel, so a batch of 500 needs 20,000 lb. Carbon already knows that — and it knows Acme doesn't keep that much on hand. Watch what happens next.

Step 4 — Carbon plans the work (the Job)

Because the order is Make to Order, Carbon creates a job — a production order that carries its own copy of the method. Acme releases job J000097 for 500 pieces and it's ready for the shop floor.

On the shop floor, operators open the MES app, clock onto each operation, and record how many good pieces they finished. That's how the "Done" statuses above got there — and how the 282 seconds of recorded labor rolled up into the job's real cost.

Step 5 — Buy the material you don't have (the Purchase Order)

Acme is short on 1-inch steel plate, so Carbon flags the shortage and Acme raises a purchase order to its steel supplier, Ryerson. Buying and making run in parallel — the steel is on its way while the shop gets set up.

When the steel arrives, a quick receipt adds it to inventory — and because A36 plate is batch-tracked, Carbon records exactly which heat of steel went into this batch of hooks. That's the start of full traceability: months later you can answer "which material went into Radiant's order?" in seconds.

Step 6 — Check it, ship it (Quality & Shipment)

The last operation on the job was Inspection — a quality gate. With the parts inspected and accepted, Acme creates a shipment, picks the 500 finished hooks, and sends them. Carbon marks the sales order line Shipped.

Step 7 — Get paid (the Invoice)

Finally, Acme bills the order. The sales invoice AR000025 pulls its lines straight from what shipped — no re-keying — and once Radiant pays, Carbon marks it Paid and posts the revenue to the books.

The whole thread, in one place

That's Carbon end to end. One part, one continuous chain of linked records — each one a tap away from the next:

Every record above linked itself to the next automatically. That's the whole idea of a system of record: enter information once, and it follows the work everywhere it goes.
