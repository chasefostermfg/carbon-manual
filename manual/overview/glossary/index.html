Glossary

Plain-English definitions of every Carbon and manufacturing term in this manual.

This page is your dictionary. Carbon (and manufacturing in general) uses a lot of words that sound technical but turn out to be simple once someone explains them in plain language. That's what this page does: one sentence per term, and wherever it helps, a real example from Acme Inc. — the demo company you'll see throughout this manual.

You don't need to read this top to bottom. Skim it once to get a feel for the vocabulary, then come back and look things up whenever a page uses a word you're not sure about. Most pages link their jargon straight here.

Where this fits. This is a reference, not a tutorial. If you're brand new, start with What is Carbon? for the big picture, then watch one real order move through the whole system in Carbon in 15 minutes. The example used in most definitions below — the Counterweight Hook CWH-A36-15 that Acme makes for Radiant Aerospace — is that same walkthrough thread.

The three letters: ERP, MES, QMS

Carbon is described as "an ERP + MES + QMS." Those three acronyms each cover one slice of running a shop, and Carbon rolls all three into a single system that shares one set of data.

TermStands forWhat it does for you
ERPEnterprise Resource PlanningThe business side — customers, suppliers, sales orders, purchase orders, invoices, inventory and costs. The system of record for what you sell, buy and own.
MESManufacturing Execution SystemThe shop-floor side — what operators see and do: clocking onto a job, recording good and scrapped pieces, following work instructions.
QMSQuality Management SystemThe quality side — inspections, non-conformances, corrective actions, gauge calibration and full traceability of what went into each part.

Because they live in one system, finishing a shop-floor operation (MES) updates the job cost (ERP) and records which material went in (QMS) — all at once, with no re-typing.

The full A–Z

Every term used in the manual, alphabetical. Real Acme examples are woven in where they make a definition click.

TermWhat it means
API keyA secret token that lets an outside program sign in to Carbon and read or change your data through the API, instead of a person clicking the screen. You create one in Settings and treat it like a password. See Using the API.
Batch / LotA group of identical units that share a history — same material, same run, same supplier delivery. Carbon's A36 steel plate is batch-tracked, so the 20,000 LB that went into job J000097 is tied to the exact delivery it came from.
Bill of Materials (BOM)The 'ingredients list' for a part — what it's made of and how much of each. The Counterweight Hook's BOM is short: 40 LB of A36 steel plate per piece. Half of a Method.
BinSee Shelf / Bin.
CAPACorrective and Preventive Action — the follow-up after a quality problem: fix what went wrong (corrective) and change the process so it doesn't happen again (preventive). Often opened from a Non-Conformance.
ConfiguratorA set of rules that builds a custom part from customer choices (size, material, finish) and prices it automatically. Acme has a 'Flame Cut Part Configurator' (FLAME CONFIG) for made-to-order plate parts.
ConsumableAn item you use up that isn't part of the finished product — gloves, coolant, sandpaper, welding wire. A type of Item. Acme stocks things like Nitrile Gloves (GLV-NIT) and Coolant (CHM-CLN).
ERPEnterprise Resource Planning — the business backbone (sales, purchasing, inventory, accounting). See the section above.
FixtureA device that holds a part in place while you work on it, so every piece is positioned the same way. A type of Item, like a Tool.
GenealogySee Traceability / Genealogy.
Gauge / CalibrationA gauge is a measuring instrument (calipers, a micrometer, a dial indicator). Calibration is checking it against a known standard on a schedule so you can trust its readings. Acme's tools include Digital Calipers (TOL-CAL) and a Micrometer (TOL-MIC).
InventoryThe physical stock you own and where it sits. Carbon tracks how many of each item are on hand, at which Location, and on which Shelf.
ItemThe master term for anything Carbon tracks: a Part, Material, Consumable, Tool, Fixture or Service. The Counterweight Hook is an item; so is the steel plate it's made from.
Item LedgerThe running log of every movement of an item in and out of inventory — receipts, issues to jobs, shipments, adjustments. It's the audit trail behind your On Hand number.
JobA production order — the instruction to actually make a quantity of a part, carrying its own copy of the Method. Acme released job J000097 to make 500 Counterweight Hooks for SO000069.
Lead TimeHow long something takes to arrive or be made, measured from when you order or start it. Used to plan when to buy material or release a job so it finishes on time.
LocationA physical site that holds inventory and runs work — a plant or warehouse. Acme has two: Headquarters and Texas. The whole hero order ran at Headquarters.
LotSee Batch / Lot.
Make to OrderA fulfillment method where you make the part only after a customer orders it. SO000069 is Make to Order, which is why ordering it caused Carbon to create job J000097.
Make to StockMaking parts ahead of demand and putting them on the shelf, so you can ship from inventory the moment an order arrives. Acme ran job J000094 to build 10,000 Thermal Exhaust Nozzles to stock.
MaterialRaw stock that gets consumed and transformed into a part — bar, plate, billet, tube. A type of Item. A36 Steel Plate 1" (A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240) is the material in the hero example.
MCP serverModel Context Protocol — a built-in interface that lets AI assistants read and operate Carbon directly, the same way a person would. This manual's Acme data was pulled through Carbon's MCP server. See MCP & AI.
MESManufacturing Execution System — the shop-floor app where operators clock onto operations and report progress. See the section above and Shop floor.
MethodThe full recipe for making a part: its Bill of Materials (what it's made of) plus its Routing (the steps to make it). The Counterweight Hook's method is one material and three operations.
MRP / PlanningMaterial Requirements Planning — Carbon's engine that looks at demand (orders, jobs) versus supply (stock, incoming POs) and tells you what to buy or make, and when. It's what flags a material shortage before you run out.
Non-Conformance (NCR)A record that something didn't meet spec — a part out of tolerance, a wrong material, a process miss. The 'NCR' (Non-Conformance Report) captures what happened so it can be dispositioned and, if needed, escalated to a CAPA.
On HandHow many units of an item you physically have available right now at a Location. The number the Item Ledger adds up to.
OperationA single step in a Routing, performed at a Work Center. Job J000097 has three operations in sequence: Flame Cut, Sandblasting, then Inspection.
OpportunityA potential piece of business that ties a quote and its eventual order together — a sales thread. SO000069 and its invoice both point back to one opportunity.
PartAn item you make or buy and sell as a finished or semi-finished product (as opposed to raw Material). A type of Item. The Counterweight Hook is a Part; the steel it's cut from is a Material.
ProcessA category of work — Flame Cut, 3-Axis CNC, Sandblasting, Quality Inspection. A Work Center is capable of one or more processes; an Operation uses a process. Acme defines 16 processes.
Pull from InventoryA line setting meaning 'take this from existing stock' rather than buying or making it fresh. On the hook's BOM, the A36 steel plate is pulled from inventory.
Purchase InvoiceA bill you receive from a supplier (also called accounts payable, prefixed AP), recording what you owe for goods or services. Acme's steel buys from Ryerson generate AP invoices such as AP000023.
Purchase Order (PO)Your formal order to a supplier — what you're buying, how much, at what price, and when you need it. Acme raised PO000057 to Ryerson for A36 steel at $0.25/LB.
QMSQuality Management System — inspections, non-conformances, calibration and traceability. See the section above and Tracking quality.
QuoteA priced offer to a customer before they commit — 'here's what that would cost.' Acme quoted 500 Counterweight Hooks to Radiant Aerospace as Q000061, then converted it to a sales order.
ReceiptThe act (and record) of accepting goods into inventory when they arrive from a supplier. Receiving against PO000057 is what adds the A36 steel to stock and starts its batch history.
Reorder PointA stock level that, when you drop to it, signals 'time to buy or make more.' One of the simple rules Carbon uses to keep stocked items from running out. See Replenishment.
ReplenishmentHow you keep an item in supply — either Make it or Buy it. Set per item. The Counterweight Hook's replenishment is Make; its steel plate's is Buy (from Ryerson).
RFQRequest for Quote — when you ask a supplier for their price before you order. The supplier's reply is a Supplier Quote. See Supplier Quote / RFQ.
RoutingThe ordered list of steps (Operations) to make a part, and where each runs. Half of a Method. The hook's routing: Flame Cut → Sandblasting → Inspection.
Sales InvoiceYour bill to a customer (accounts receivable, prefixed AR), built from what shipped. AR000025 billed Radiant Aerospace $36,820 for the 500 hooks and was marked Paid.
Sales Order (SO)A customer's firm commitment to buy — the anchor that the job, shipment and invoice all hang off. SO000069 locked in 500 hooks at $73.14 each for Radiant Aerospace.
SerialA unique number assigned to a single individual unit, so it can be tracked one piece at a time (versus a Batch, which tracks a group). Used for high-value or regulated parts.
ServiceWork you sell or buy that isn't a physical thing — outside plating, an inspection, a subcontracted operation. A type of Item. Acme's suppliers include service providers like Allen's Anodizing.
Shelf / BinThe specific spot inside a Location where stock physically lives — a labelled shelf, bin or rack position. Acme's A36 steel sits in bin R02-B01-01 at Headquarters.
ShipmentThe record of goods leaving your dock to a customer — what was picked, how many, and to whom. The 500 finished hooks shipped to Radiant on a shipment linked to SO000069 and invoice AR000025.
SupplierA company you buy from. Acme's primary steel supplier is Ryerson; others include McMaster-Carr, Fastenal and Grainger. See Suppliers.
Supplier Quote / RFQA price you've requested from (and received from) a supplier before committing to a purchase order. You send an RFQ; the answer is a supplier quote you can turn into a PO.
ToolAn instrument used to perform work that isn't consumed into the product — a drill, a wrench, a caliper. A type of Item. Acme tracks tools like the Cordless Drill (TOL-CDL) and Torque Wrench (TOL-TRQ).
Traceability / GenealogyBeing able to answer 'exactly what went into this part, and where did it go?' Because the hook's steel is batch-tracked, Acme can trace Radiant's 500 hooks back to the specific steel delivery — and forward to the shipment.
UoMUnit of Measure — how you count or weigh an item: EA (each), LB (pounds), FT, and so on. The Counterweight Hook is counted in EA; its steel is consumed in LB.
Work CenterA specific place or machine where Operations happen, with its own labor and machine cost rates. The hook's Flame Cut ran at 'Burn Table — PA2'; its Inspection at the 'Inspection Station.'
